# Defining Sustainability

Sustainability is not one universal target. It depends on the system, timescale, community, and tradeoffs your group chooses to make visible.

For this summit, define sustainability by naming what is being sustained, for whom, and at what cost:

- **Ecological sustainability:** maintaining or recovering ecosystem function, biodiversity, habitat, water, soil, or climate resilience.
- **Social sustainability:** supporting health, safety, equity, access, cultural values, livelihoods, or community agency.
- **Technical sustainability:** making tools, data products, and workflows maintainable after the workshop ends.
- **Institutional sustainability:** fitting outputs into real decision processes, governance, budgets, and long-term stewardship.
- **Computational sustainability:** accounting for energy use, compute cost, storage, model reuse, and avoidable waste.

## Questions to Answer

- What is the system your project is trying to sustain?
- Who benefits if the work succeeds?
- Who might bear the cost?
- What tradeoffs are visible, and what tradeoffs are still hidden?
- What evidence would show that the outcome is actually more sustainable?
