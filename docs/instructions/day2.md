# Day 2 — Build, Explore, and Apply New Skills

## What today is about

Today is about turning a promising direction into a working analysis. Keep editing the [Home page](../index.md). Use the [Example page](../example.md) only as a completed model.

By the end of the day, the Home page should show a sharper question, a clearer workflow, at least one emerging result, and a two-minute report out.

[Edit the Day 2 sections](https://github.com/CU-ESIIL/Project_group_OASIS/edit/main/docs/index.md){ .md-button .md-button--primary }

## Summit Report Out workflow

Your group page is both your workspace and your final report-out page. During the summit, use the edit buttons to update the sections for each day. On Day 3, use the **Summit Report Out** button to view the page in a clean Summit Report Out layout.

For the final report out, focus on:

- What question or challenge your group worked on
- What you built, tested, mapped, wrote, or designed
- What changed in your thinking
- What someone else could do next
- Images that make the work easier to understand

## Reconnect with your question

Start by rereading [Project Question](../index.md#project-question).

Ask:

- Does the question still make sense?
- Did yesterday's data exploration change what matters?
- Do we need to narrow, sharpen, or redirect?
- What would be enough evidence for a Day 3 claim?

Update the Home page if the question has changed.

## Use the specialty tracks

Use [Specialty Tracks and Strategy](../index.md#specialty-tracks-and-strategy) to record how the group used summit trainings.

Capture:

- who attended which track
- what each person brought back
- what new method, tool, or idea the group tried
- what changed in the project because of the training

## Push the data exploration further

Use [Data Exploration](../index.md#data-exploration) to add a clearer plot, map, table, or summary.

Helpful prompts:

- What pattern are we looking for?
- What comparison would help?
- What variable, place, time period, or group matters most?
- What would make this figure easier for someone else to understand?

## Document methods and code

Use [Methods and Code](../index.md#methods-and-code) to explain the workflow simply enough that someone else could repeat it.

Include:

- what data you used
- what tool or notebook you used
- what processing or analysis step mattered most
- where the code lives
- what still needs cleanup

## Start forming results

Use [Results](../index.md#results) to write early findings, patterns, surprises, or problems.

Useful result statements can sound like:

- We are starting to see...
- One pattern that surprised us was...
- This result is still uncertain because...
- The data do not yet support...

## Report Out (2 minutes)

Focus: synthesis, not detail.

Your goal is to explain what you did and what you are starting to see. Add this to [Report Out (Day 2)](../index.md#report-out-day2).

Structure:

- **Why:** What question are you trying to answer?
- **What:** What data or approach did you use?
- **Early finding:** What are you seeing so far?

Prompts:

- What is the one thing your group is trying to figure out?
- What did you try?
- What surprised you?

What good looks like:

> We are testing how perimeter geometry affects fire growth using event-level data.

## End-of-day check

Before leaving Day 2, make sure the Home page has:

- an updated or reaffirmed question
- a note about how the group used the specialty tracks
- clearer data exploration
- a methods note
- at least one emerging result
- a two-minute report out draft
